Our Story

The Vietnam Team HBV was founded by Tien Minh Nguyen, a Vietnamese immigrant student at Menlo Atherton High School in Menlo Park, California. Tien Minh's passion for social change began in middle school when he started volunteering at Stanford University's Asian Liver Center, the first non-profit organization in the United States addressing the disproportionately high rates of chronic hepatitis B infection and liver cancer in Asians and Asian Americans. It was here that he learned about the devastating impact of hepatitis B and liver cancer on Asian communities, particularly Vietnamese individuals.

With a deepening commitment to this cause, Tien Minh engaged in both community education and research activities at the Asian Liver Center. He was shocked to learn that 1 in 10 Vietnamese individuals live with chronic hepatitis B, a leading cause of liver cancer, and that Vietnam ranked the 5th in liver cancer death in the world. Inspired to make a difference, Tien Minh decided to bring this initiative to Vietnam, his home country. He reached out to his peers in Vietnam and networked with students who shared his passion for making a difference in their community. By December 2022, the Vietnam team HBV had built a network of 9 high school chapters across the country, with 25 core members. Our members are dedicated to learning and using our skills to promote a healthier society. We are proud to join the global JoinJade campaign initiated by Stanford University’s Asian Liver Center in raising awareness about hepatitis B and liver cancer, providing education and resources, and supporting research to help combat these diseases. Thank you for joining us in this important mission.

Our Clubs

Vietnam HBV TEAM Club is a member of the global TEAM HBV network, a multinational network of students participating in the #JOINJADE program initiated by Stanford University with the goal of spreading information, eliminating hepatitis B and liver cancer globally. We are the youth branch of the Stanford University’s Asian Liver Center, the first non-profit in the United States that addresses the high incidence and the dangers of hepatitis B and liver cancer in Asians. Vietnam Team HBV works under the umbrella of the global JOINJADE campaign and in conjunction with the Team HBV High School in the United States, Australia and China for collaboration and ideas exchange.
