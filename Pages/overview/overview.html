Our Mission

Vietnam Team HBV gathers high school students who are passionate about being future leaders, using sciences and technologies to create long-lasting impacts on the community. Our mission is to engage and empower students in spreading information and taking actions to eradicate hepatitis B and liver cancer. We work to increase awareness of the dangers of hepatitis B and liver cancer, the ideals of the JoinJade Campaign, and provide information to better the health of the student body and the local community. Viêm gan B tưởng chừng là một căn bệnh thường gặp, ít nguy hiểm đến tính mạng nhưng thực chất căn bệnh này ảnh hưởng đến khoảng 296 triệu người, gây ra khoảng 820 000 ca tử vong hằng năm và 25% trường hợp nhiễm viêm gan B mãn tính tiến triển thành ung thư gan. Việt Nam nằm trong vùng có tỷ lệ nhiễm virus viêm gan cao trên thế giới. Nhận biết được tầm quan trọng của việc phòng chống bệnh viêm gan B đối với sức khỏe cộng đồng Việt Nam, Vietnam Team HBV đã được ra đời. Vietnam Team HBV hoạt động dưới sự bảo trợ của chiến dịch JOINJADE - một chiến dịch toàn cầu nhằm chống lại sự kỳ thị, chấm dứt sự lây truyền và loại bỏ những trường hợp tử vong liên quan đến bệnh viêm gan B - và kết hợp với Team HBV tại Hoa Kỳ, Úc và Trung Quốc để hợp tác và trao đổi ý tưởng. Chúng tôi hứa hẹn sẽ tiếp nối sứ mệnh cao cả và đem lại nhiều giá trị tích cực cho cộng đồng trong việc nhận thức về căn bệnh.

Our Story

The Vietnam Team HBV was founded by Tien Minh Nguyen, a Vietnamese immigrant student at Menlo Atherton High School in Menlo Park, California. Tien Minh's passion for social change began in middle school when he started volunteering at Stanford University's Asian Liver Center, the first non-profit organization in the United States addressing the disproportionately high rates of chronic hepatitis B infection and liver cancer in Asians and Asian Americans. It was here that he learned about the devastating impact of hepatitis B and liver cancer on Asian communities, particularly Vietnamese individuals. With a deepening commitment to this cause, Tien Minh engaged in both community education and research activities at the Asian Liver Center. He was shocked to learn that 1 in 10 Vietnamese individuals live with chronic hepatitis B, a leading cause of liver cancer, and that Vietnam ranked the 5th in liver cancer death in the world. Inspired to make a difference, Tien Minh decided to bring this initiative to Vietnam, his home country. He reached out to his peers in Vietnam and networked with students who shared his passion for making a difference in their community. By December 2022, the Vietnam team HBV had built a network of 9 high school chapters across the country, with 25 core members. Our members are dedicated to learning and using our skills to promote a healthier society. We are proud to join the global JoinJade campaign initiated by Stanford University’s Asian Liver Center in raising awareness about hepatitis B and liver cancer, providing education and resources, and supporting research to help combat these diseases. Thank you for joining us in this important mission.

Why Hepatitis B and Liver Cancer?

  • 1 in 10 Vietnamese are infected with chronic hepatitis B. Most of them don’t have symptoms and don't know about their infection.
  • Without timely treatment, 1 in 4 patients with chronic hepatitis B will die because of liver cancer or liver cirrhosis.
  • Vietnam ranks number 5 in the world in liver cancer rate.
  • Hepatitis B is vaccine-preventable and treatable.
  • Hepatitis B is a much neglected health issue in Vietnam.
  • The World Health Organization’s theme for World Hepatitis Day in 2022 is “HEPATITIS B CAN’T WAIT”

    Affiliation

    Vietnam HBV TEAM Club is a member of the global TEAM HBV network, a multinational network of students participating in the #JOINJADE program initiated by Stanford University with the goal of spreading information, eliminating hepatitis B and liver cancer globally. We are the youth branch of the Stanford University’s Asian Liver Center, the first non-profit in the United States that addresses the high incidence and the dangers of hepatitis B and liver cancer in Asians. Vietnam Team HBV works under the umbrella of the global JOINJADE campaign and in conjunction with the Team HBV High School in the United States, Australia and China for collaboration and ideas exchange.
